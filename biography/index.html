Biography

Early Life

Yevgenij Zamyatin was born in Lebedjan, 300 km (186 mi) south of Moscow, from a Russian Orthodox priest and schoolmaster, and a musician.

He studied engineering for the Imperial Russian Navy in Saint Petersburg, from 1902 until 1908, becoming, in the meantime, an Atheist and a Marxist.

He was arrested twice: in 1905 and in 1911. After illegally escaping the first exile, he was exiled again and started writing fiction as a hobby.

Amnestied in 1913 for 300-years of rule by the House of Romanov, he graduated as an engineer and started working.
